Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about Rumbo.

How do I request a ride on Rumbo?
Open the app, enter your destination, and select a vehicle option. Confirm your pickup location and request the ride. You'll be matched with a nearby driver.
Are the drivers on Rumbo licensed and vetted?
Yes, all Rumbo drivers are licensed and undergo a thorough vetting process, including background checks, to ensure your safety and security.
Can I request a specific driver on Rumbo?
No, you cannot request a specific driver on Rumbo. Rumbo's driver allocation system assigns available drivers to rides based on their proximity and availability.
Is there a maximum number of passengers allowed in a single ride?
Yes, there is typically a maximum number of passengers allowed in a single ride with Rumbo. The maximum number of passengers may vary depending on the type of vehicle you choose when booking a ride.
What should I do if I feel unsafe during a ride?
If you feel unsafe during a Rumbo ride:

Contact the Driver: Talk to the driver about your concerns if you can

Use the Emergency Button: Use the app's emergency feature to notify Rumbo

Share Location: Share your location with someone you trust

Call Emergency Services: If in immediate danger, call local emergency services (e.g. 10111)

Exit Safely: If necessary, exit the vehicle in a safe location

Report to Rumbo: Contact Rumbo support through the app or website to report the issue
Are driver ratings visible to passengers?
Yes, driver ratings are visible to passengers using Rumbo. When you request a ride, you can see the driver's name, profile picture, and their average rating based on feedback from previous passengers.
